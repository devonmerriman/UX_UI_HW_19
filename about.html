HOW I BECAME A DESIGNER



I used to work for the U.S Forest Service as a science communicator, where I wrote and edited documents in plain language. But the assignment that truly excited me was designing several pages of the agencyâ€™s website. Many government site struggle to be user-friendly, and the Forest Service was no exception. There was nothing I wanted more than to reorganize their entire navigation and bring more visuals into the overall design. I never got the chance to carry that out in real life, but I designed a mockup anyway

I realized that science communication was only a small part of what I could do. I wanted to make learning anything online accessible, easy, and fun. I began to hone my UX and UI skills by working for StoryWorld, an online language learning program for children. You can find my case study about StoryWorld here

Looking back, the fact that I landed in design makes a lot of sense. My goal with science communication was to make the world easier to understand, and despite a change in focus, that goal remains. I now look forward to a lifetime of learning, simplifying, and problem-solving as a designer.
